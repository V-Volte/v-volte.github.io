My Works

Love and grief

They fall silent at the setting of the sun,
The sparrow song beside my window ken
Leaves rustling in the spring zephyrs
The cries and shouts of childhood joy
Hang in the air and numb my thought

While the Sun still shines so warm,
Happier times and thoughts appear,
And for a while the distant call
Falls silent, vanquished by the day.

I dread the setting of the sun,
The dusk and the colden night;
Alone with naught but darkness
Dark thoughts rise and torment again

The howling of a distant wolf
The silver light upon my window ken
Leaves rustling in a demon’s breath
Sharpen and darken perilous thought.

Dark? Not dark! It is not dark,
The remembrance of the lost;
There can be no grief where love was not,
A stronger love, a fiercer grief.

I must rejoice then when grief visits
In the still silence of the starless night
For this is the price, and this the proof,
That I have loved and was beloved.

O maiden rosy hued, thou bringer of the light
Each day art born anew and brighter still;
The Sky himself does redden at thy sight,
As him thou fillest with thy radiant golden shine.

Thy soft and gladdening rays each morn do shine
Upon the hidden beauty of the world,
That by thy sister’s gloomy hues consumed,
Now freed, comes forth in fiercer fairness to the light.

The myriad stars that do adorn the gaze of Night
Are vanquished in thy brilliant golden glow;
The silver Moon into the West recedes
As far outshone by thee of radiant roses crowned.

The clouds each Morning in thy lustrous smile rejoice,
As thy illuming rays suffuse the skies;
Reflecting thy amourous shine they forge
In rosy light a path to greet the rising Sun.

And him thou maiden fair dost lead across the sky
And givest in at noon to his embrace;
And the next morn arisest brighter still,
Heralded by the loving Eosphoric wind.
